It is this focus on complexity that leads into one of the defining facets of Giliane’s work – her use of pattern. The vast majority of Giliane's projects are defined by their busy, overcrowded aesthetic, and the designer's real skill lies in her intuitive ways of making these pieces make sense. This approach is apparent in her posters for the RKC concert hall in Switzerland. Close up, the posters look like a pleasing, yet slightly incomprehensible set of dense patterns; it is only upon moving further away from the posters that you begin to see the outline of the letters 'R', 'C' and 'K' emerge.
